Ready to See the Numbers?

Parent PLUS loans affect the entire family's financial picture. The calculator at ItsYourIncome.com helps you model different scenarios.

What You'll Need

  • Total Parent PLUS loan amount (or planned amount)

Scenarios You Can Model

Before Borrowing

  • What if we borrow $40,000 vs $20,000?
  • How does this affect our retirement savings?

  • What if our child goes to a less expensive school?

During Repayment

  • What if the student contributes $200/month?
  • Should we use standard or extended repayment?
  • Is consolidation and ICR worth it?
  • Should we refinance to a lower rate?

Family Arrangements

  • Parent pays for 5 years, then student takes over
  • 50/50 split from day one
  • Parent pays minimum, student pays extra
  • Student pays once income exceeds $50,000

Comparing Parent PLUS to Alternatives

The calculator helps you compare:

  • Parent PLUS loans – Higher interest, parent's responsibility
  • Private student loans – May require co-signer, student's responsibility
  • Home equity loan – Lower interest, but home is collateral
  • Student working part-time – No debt, but less time for studies

Understanding the True Cost

Parent PLUS loans are expensive. The calculator shows you:

Example: $40,000 Parent PLUS Loan

At 8% interest over 10 years:

  • Monthly payment: $485
  • Total paid: $58,200
  • Interest paid: $18,200

If extended to 25 years:

  • Monthly payment: $309
  • Total paid: $92,700
  • Interest paid: $52,700

Difference: $34,500 more in interest for lower monthly payments

Impact on Retirement

The calculator can show how Parent PLUS loans affect retirement savings:

  • If you're 50 and borrow $40,000, you'll be paying until age 60 (or 75 on extended plan)
  • That's money not going into retirement accounts
  • Lost investment growth compounds over time

Parent PLUS Repayment Options

Understanding your choices

When Repayment Starts

Parent PLUS loans enter repayment as soon as the loan is fully disbursed.

However, parents can request a deferment while the student is enrolled at least half-time, plus an additional 6 months after the student graduates or drops below half-time.

Important

Interest accrues during deferment. If you don't pay it, it will capitalize when deferment ends, increasing your total balance.

Standard Repayment Plan

This is the default option.

How It Works

  • Fixed monthly payment
  • 10-year repayment term
  • Pays off the loan fastest
  • Minimizes total interest paid

Example

$40,000 Parent PLUS loan at 8% interest:

  • Monthly payment: ~$485
  • Total paid over 10 years: ~$58,200
  • Total interest: ~$18,200

Who It's For

Parents who can afford the monthly payment and want to pay off the loan as quickly as possible.

Graduated Repayment Plan

Payments start low and increase every 2 years.

How It Works

  • Payments start lower than standard
  • Increase every 2 years
  • Still a 10-year term
  • Costs more in interest than standard

Who It's For

Parents who expect their income to increase over time and need lower payments now.

Extended Repayment Plan

Stretches repayment to 25 years.

How It Works

  • Fixed or graduated payments
  • 25-year term
  • Lower monthly payment
  • Significantly more interest paid

Example

$40,000 Parent PLUS loan at 8% interest:

  • Monthly payment: ~$309
  • Total paid over 25 years: ~$92,700
  • Total interest: ~$52,700

Who It's For

Parents who need the lowest possible monthly payment and are willing to pay significantly more in interest.

Income-Contingent Repayment (ICR)

The only income-driven option for Parent PLUS loans, but it requires consolidation first.

How It Works

  • Must consolidate Parent PLUS loans into a Direct Consolidation Loan
  • Payment is 20% of discretionary income
  • Remaining balance forgiven after 25 years
  • Forgiven amount is taxable income

The Catch

ICR payments are often higher than standard repayment because they're based on the parent's income, not the student's.

It's most useful for parents with low income relative to their debt.

Example

Parent with $50,000 income and $40,000 in Parent PLUS loans:

  • Monthly payment: ~$300-400 (varies by family size)
  • After 25 years, remaining balance is forgiven
  • Forgiven amount is taxed as income

Consolidation

You can consolidate multiple Parent PLUS loans into a single Direct Consolidation Loan.

Benefits

  • Single monthly payment
  • Access to ICR repayment plan
  • Potential eligibility for Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

Drawbacks

  • Unpaid interest capitalizes
  • Weighted average interest rate (rounded up to nearest 1/8%)
  • Resets the clock on PSLF progress (if you had any)

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

Parent PLUS loans can qualify for PSLF, but only after consolidation.

Requirements

  • Consolidate into a Direct Consolidation Loan
  • Enroll in ICR repayment
  • Work full-time for a qualifying employer (government or nonprofit)
  • Make 120 qualifying payments

Who It's For

Parents who work in public service and have high debt relative to income.

Deferment and Forbearance

Deferment

Temporarily pause payments while the student is in school or during economic hardship.

Interest continues to accrue.

Forbearance

Temporarily pause or reduce payments due to financial difficulty.

Interest continues to accrue.

Limited to 12 months at a time, 3 years total.

Use Sparingly

Deferment and forbearance increase your total debt because interest keeps accruing. Use them only when absolutely necessary.

Should You Refinance?

Private lenders offer refinancing for Parent PLUS loans, often at lower interest rates.

Benefits

  • Lower interest rate (if you qualify)
  • Lower monthly payment or faster payoff
  • Can transfer loan to student's name (some lenders)

Drawbacks

  • Lose federal protections (deferment, forbearance, forgiveness)
  • Requires good credit
  • Variable rates can increase over time

Parent PLUS Loans FAQ

Common Questions About Parent PLUS Loans

Get answers about eligibility, repayment, and alternatives to Parent PLUS loans.

Who is responsible for repaying Parent PLUS loans?

The parent borrower is legally responsible, not the student. The debt appears on the parent's credit report and cannot be transferred to the student.

What are the credit requirements?

Parents cannot have an adverse credit history, defined as:

  • 90+ days delinquent on debts over $2,085
  • Accounts in collections or charge-offs
  • Repossession, foreclosure, or bankruptcy (past 5 years)
  • Wage garnishment or tax liens

What is the interest rate?

Parent PLUS loans have fixed interest rates set annually by Congress. For 2024-2025, the rate is 9.08%. There's also a 4.228% origination fee deducted from each disbursement.

Can Parent PLUS loans be forgiven?

Parent PLUS loans are not eligible for most federal forgiveness programs. However, they may qualify for Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) if:

  • Parent works for a qualifying employer
  • Loans are consolidated into a Direct Consolidation Loan
  • Enrolled in Income-Contingent Repayment (ICR)

What are alternatives to Parent PLUS loans?

  • Maximize student federal loans first
  • Private student loans (student's name, parent cosigns)
  • Home equity loans or lines of credit
  • 529 plan withdrawals
  • School payment plans
  • Student works part-time or attends less expensive school

Can I transfer Parent PLUS loans to my child?

No, federal Parent PLUS loans cannot be transferred. Some private lenders offer refinancing that allows the student to assume the debt, but this means losing federal protections.

What repayment options are available?

  • Standard 10-year repayment
  • Graduated repayment
  • Extended repayment (25 years)
  • Income-Contingent Repayment (ICR) after consolidation

Note: Parent PLUS loans don't qualify for most income-driven plans.
